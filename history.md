## The Early Days (1950s–1980s)
#### Expert Systems: 
Rule-based architectures that used hardcoded "if-then" logic to solve problems in narrow domains (like early medical diagnosis).
#### Perceptrons & Multilayer Perceptrons (MLPs): 
The earliest artificial neural networks. They started as simple single-layer classifiers and evolved into the multi-layered foundation of all deep learning.

## The Deep Learning Boom (2010s)
#### Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): 
Architectures specialized for grid-like data. They use filters to recognize spatial patterns, kicking off the modern computer vision revolution (e.g., AlexNet, ResNet).
#### Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) & LSTMs: 
Architectures built to process sequential data (like speech or text) step-by-step. Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks specifically helped these models remember past inputs over longer sequences.

## The Generative Era (2014–Present)
#### Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs): 
A dual-network setup where a "generator" creates fake data and a "discriminator" tries to catch the fakes, resulting in highly realistic image generation.
#### Diffusion Models: 
The architecture behind modern AI art tools (like Midjourney or Stable Diffusion), which generates data by learning how to gradually reverse and remove "noise" from an image.
#### Graph Neural Networks (GNNs): 
Architectures designed to handle complex, interconnected data, such as social networks, traffic systems, or molecular structures.

## Modern Sequence Titans (2017–Present)
#### Transformers: 
The architecture that introduced "self-attention" to process entire sequences in parallel. It is the engine behind almost all modern Large Language Models (LLMs).
#### State Space Models (SSMs) / Mamba: 
The highly efficient, linear-scaling architectures we discussed earlier, designed to handle massive context windows without the heavy computational cost of Transformers.
#### Mixture-of-Experts (MoE): 
A routing architecture that selectively activates only a small subset of "expert" neural networks for any given prompt, drastically reducing compute costs for massive models.


## The Emerging Frontier
#### Neuro-Symbolic AI: 
Hybrid architectures that combine the data-driven pattern recognition of modern neural networks with the strict, auditable logic of early rule-based systems.**
