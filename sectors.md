## Natural Language Processing (Text)
#### Transformers: 
The undisputed leaders for modern text generation, translation, and complex reasoning. They power Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-3 and BERT.
#### Mamba / State Space Models (SSMs): 
A highly efficient emerging alternative to Transformers, excelling at processing massive text documents or codebases without running out of memory.
#### RNNs & LSTMs: 
The historical standard for text processing, operating step-by-step to predict sequential words, though mostly replaced by Transformers today.

## Computer Vision (Images & Video)
#### Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): 
The foundational architecture for image recognition, object detection, and medical image analysis (e.g., ResNet, U-Net).
#### Vision Transformers (ViT): 
An adaptation of the text-based Transformer that divides images into patches, now rivaling CNNs for image classification.
#### GANs & Diffusion Models: 
The primary architectures for generating images. GANs create highly realistic photos (like DeepFakes), while Diffusion models power text-to-image tools like DALL-E and Midjourney.

## Speech and Audio
#### RNNs & LSTMs: 
Long used for automatic speech recognition (transcribing spoken words into text) and voice assistants.
#### Mamba: 
Increasingly used for tasks like speech separation (isolating voices in a crowded room) and speech enhancement, as it can process long, continuous audio signals very efficiently.

## Complex Interconnected Data (Graphs)
#### Graph Neural Networks (GNNs): 
Architectures specifically built to process data that does not fit into a grid or sequence, such as social networks, traffic systems, or chemical molecule structures.

##  Multimodal (All of the Above)
#### Unified Multimodal Transformers: 
Cutting-edge models like Google's Gemini or OpenAI's GPT-4o combine text, vision, and audio processing into a single architecture to understand multiple data types simultaneously.
